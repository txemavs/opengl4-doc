function `glBegin`
------------------

.. source file man/glBegin

.. currentmodule:: OpenGL.GL

Delimit the vertices of a primitive or a group of like primitives

.. c:function:: glBegin
.. c:function:: void glBegin(GLenum  mode)
   :noindex: 


   
   :param mode:
      Specifies the primitive or primitives that will be created from vertices presented between :c:func:`glBegin` and the subsequent :c:func:`glEnd`. Ten symbolic constants are accepted: ``GL_POINTS``, ``GL_LINES``, ``GL_LINE_STRIP``, ``GL_LINE_LOOP``, ``GL_TRIANGLES``, ``GL_TRIANGLE_STRIP``, ``GL_TRIANGLE_FAN``, ``GL_QUADS``, ``GL_QUAD_STRIP``, and ``GL_POLYGON``.
   
.. c:function:: void glEnd( void)

.. seealso::

.. rubric:: Description


:c:func:`glBegin` and :c:func:`glEnd` delimit the vertices that define a primitive or a group of like primitives. :c:func:`glBegin` accepts a single argument that specifies in which of ten ways the vertices are interpreted. Taking |EQUATION_1_| as an integer count starting at one, and |EQUATION_2_| as the total number of vertices specified, the interpretations are as follows:


   
   ``GL_POINTS``
      Treats each vertex as a single point. Vertex |EQUATION_3_| defines point |EQUATION_4_| . |EQUATION_5_| points are drawn.
   
   ``GL_LINES``
      Treats each pair of vertices as an independent line segment. Vertices |EQUATION_6_| and |EQUATION_7_| define line |EQUATION_8_| . |EQUATION_9_| lines are drawn.
   
   ``GL_LINE_STRIP``
      Draws a connected group of line segments from the first vertex to the last. Vertices |EQUATION_10_| and |EQUATION_11_| define line |EQUATION_12_| . |EQUATION_13_| lines are drawn.
   
   ``GL_LINE_LOOP``
      Draws a connected group of line segments from the first vertex to the last, then back to the first. Vertices |EQUATION_14_| and |EQUATION_15_| define line |EQUATION_16_| . The last line, however, is defined by vertices |EQUATION_17_| and |EQUATION_18_| . |EQUATION_19_| lines are drawn.
   
   ``GL_TRIANGLES``
      Treats each triplet of vertices as an independent triangle. Vertices |EQUATION_20_| , |EQUATION_21_| , and |EQUATION_22_| define triangle |EQUATION_23_| . |EQUATION_24_| triangles are drawn.
   
   ``GL_TRIANGLE_STRIP``
      Draws a connected group of triangles. One triangle is defined for each vertex presented after the first two vertices. For odd |EQUATION_25_| , vertices |EQUATION_26_| , |EQUATION_27_| , and |EQUATION_28_| define triangle |EQUATION_29_| . For even |EQUATION_30_| , vertices |EQUATION_31_| , |EQUATION_32_| , and |EQUATION_33_| define triangle |EQUATION_34_| . |EQUATION_35_| triangles are drawn.
   
   ``GL_TRIANGLE_FAN``
      Draws a connected group of triangles. One triangle is defined for each vertex presented after the first two vertices. Vertices |EQUATION_36_| , |EQUATION_37_| , and |EQUATION_38_| define triangle |EQUATION_39_| . |EQUATION_40_| triangles are drawn.
   
   ``GL_QUADS``
      Treats each group of four vertices as an independent quadrilateral. Vertices |EQUATION_41_| , |EQUATION_42_| , |EQUATION_43_| , and |EQUATION_44_| define quadrilateral |EQUATION_45_| . |EQUATION_46_| quadrilaterals are drawn.
   
   ``GL_QUAD_STRIP``
      Draws a connected group of quadrilaterals. One quadrilateral is defined for each pair of vertices presented after the first pair. Vertices |EQUATION_47_| , |EQUATION_48_| , |EQUATION_49_| , and |EQUATION_50_| define quadrilateral |EQUATION_51_| . |EQUATION_52_| quadrilaterals are drawn. Note that the order in which vertices are used to construct a quadrilateral from strip data is different from that used with independent data.
   
   ``GL_POLYGON``
      Draws a single, convex polygon. Vertices |EQUATION_53_| through |EQUATION_54_| define this polygon.
   
Only a subset of GL commands can be used between :c:func:`glBegin` and :c:func:`glEnd`. The commands are :c:func:`glVertex`, :c:func:`glColor`, :c:func:`glSecondaryColor`, :c:func:`glIndex`, :c:func:`glNormal`, :c:func:`glFogCoord`, :c:func:`glTexCoord`, :c:func:`glMultiTexCoord`, :c:func:`glVertexAttrib`, :c:func:`glEvalCoord`, :c:func:`glEvalPoint`, :c:func:`glArrayElement`, :c:func:`glMaterial`, and :c:func:`glEdgeFlag`. Also, it is acceptable to use :c:func:`glCallList` or :c:func:`glCallLists` to execute display lists that include only the preceding commands. If any other GL command is executed between :c:func:`glBegin` and :c:func:`glEnd`, the error flag is set and the command is ignored.

Regardless of the value chosen for *mode* , there is no limit to the number of vertices that can be defined between :c:func:`glBegin` and :c:func:`glEnd`. Lines, triangles, quadrilaterals, and polygons that are incompletely specified are not drawn. Incomplete specification results when either too few vertices are provided to specify even a single primitive or when an incorrect multiple of vertices is specified. The incomplete primitive is ignored; the rest are drawn.

The minimum specification of vertices for each primitive is as follows: 1 for a point, 2 for a line, 3 for a triangle, 4 for a quadrilateral, and 3 for a polygon. Modes that require a certain multiple of vertices are ``GL_LINES`` (2), ``GL_TRIANGLES`` (3), ``GL_QUADS`` (4), and ``GL_QUAD_STRIP`` (2).


**Errors:**

   * ``GL_INVALID_ENUM`` is generated if *mode* is set to an unaccepted value.
   * ``GL_INVALID_OPERATION`` is generated if :c:func:`glBegin` is executed between a :c:func:`glBegin` and the corresponding execution of :c:func:`glEnd`.
   * ``GL_INVALID_OPERATION`` is generated if :c:func:`glEnd` is executed without being preceded by a :c:func:`glBegin`.
   * ``GL_INVALID_OPERATION`` is generated if a command other than :c:func:`glVertex`, :c:func:`glColor`, :c:func:`glSecondaryColor`, :c:func:`glIndex`, :c:func:`glNormal`, :c:func:`glFogCoord`, :c:func:`glTexCoord`, :c:func:`glMultiTexCoord`, :c:func:`glVertexAttrib`, :c:func:`glEvalCoord`, :c:func:`glEvalPoint`, :c:func:`glArrayElement`, :c:func:`glMaterial`, :c:func:`glEdgeFlag`, :c:func:`glCallList`, or :c:func:`glCallLists` is executed between the execution of :c:func:`glBegin` and the corresponding execution :c:func:`glEnd`.
   * Execution of :c:func:`glEnableClientState`, :c:func:`glDisableClientState`, :c:func:`glEdgeFlagPointer`, :c:func:`glFogCoordPointer`, :c:func:`glTexCoordPointer`, :c:func:`glColorPointer`, :c:func:`glSecondaryColorPointer`, :c:func:`glIndexPointer`, :c:func:`glNormalPointer`, :c:func:`glVertexPointer`, :c:func:`glVertexAttribPointer`, :c:func:`glInterleavedArrays`, or :c:func:`glPixelStore` is not allowed after a call to :c:func:`glBegin` and before the corresponding call to :c:func:`glEnd`, but an error may or may not be generated.
